About me:

I hail from a small, Pennsylvania-Dutch dominated region of the world called Lancaster County. I'm not Pennsylvania-Dutch but actually come from Asian ancestry, hence the surname Ong.

I like to write, I like to do math, and I love meeting new people and trying new things. I also feel very comfortable in front of crowd should the need ever arise.

I studied Mechanical Engineering at McGill University in Montreal, but my interests don't stop at springs and levers and supersonic jet engines. Linguistics and writing/communication are some of my other passions, among functional fitness, fermentation, and faith.

Some of my personal projects at the moment include:

  • Baby's First Bessel, a children’s book about the use of Bessel functions on the playground
  • Jack the Jet Engine, a children’s book about the mechanisms of turbofan machinery
  • Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) applications for fish shoaling and pedestrian behavior
  • High-velocity impact dynamics simulations with ANSYS Workbench

And until recently, I had been working on:

  • The design of a Scaled Mock Nuclear fUsion Reactor Facility (SMURF) for my capstone design project
  • Blood flow analysis in the Iliac Bifurcation using ANSYS Fluent
  • Literature review on the applications of seismic metamaterial cloaking using phononic crystals
  • Cabbage ferments including sauerkraut and kimchi, and a water kefir ferment
